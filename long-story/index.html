Once there was a dwarf,
Who worked all day and night with his daughter and wife to support.

He works with his hands, digging ditches and soldering,
In the sun, rain, and snow, resolution never wavering.

Though he enjoys company, he’d rather be alone,
“One makes less of a mess than more” He groans.

And although he likes the pay, he doesn’t appreciate
The back-breaking work only a day off can alleviate.

But in the end, he comes home and sighs
With a soft bed and a warm meal to heal his weary cries.
